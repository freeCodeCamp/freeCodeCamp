# --description--

Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini are powerful tools that have become increasingly accessible to developers and learners. While these tools can significantly enhance your learning experience, using them incorrectly can hinder your understanding and skill development.

First, let's understand what AI and LLMs can do well. They excel at explaining complex concepts, generating code examples, helping debug errors, and providing alternative perspectives on problems. When used thoughtfully, they can accelerate your learning by helping you overcome obstacles quickly.

However, there are important limitations and risks to be aware of. LLMs can generate incorrect information confidently—a phenomenon known as "hallucination." They may provide outdated information, especially regarding rapidly evolving technologies. Their responses can lack nuance or context that a human mentor would provide.

Here are guidelines for effective use:

**Use AI tools to supplement, not replace, your learning.** When you encounter a difficult concept, try to understand it yourself first through the freeCodeCamp curriculum, documentation, and hands-on practice. Use AI to clarify specific points of confusion after you've made genuine effort.

**Always verify AI-generated information.** Don't blindly trust code or explanations. Test code examples, cross-reference with official documentation, and understand *why* something works, not just *that* it works.

**Be cautious with assessments and projects.** The goal of freeCodeCamp is to build your skills. Copying AI-generated solutions without understanding them defeats this purpose. Use AI for guidance and hints, not complete answers.

**Develop critical thinking.** Ask AI tools to explain their reasoning, question their suggestions, and use them as a starting point for deeper exploration rather than a final answer.

**Understand the tool's limitations.** LLMs don't truly "understand" code—they predict likely responses based on patterns in their training data. They can miss logical errors or produce code that appears correct but doesn't solve the actual problem.

**Use AI for learning, not bypassing learning.** The goal isn't to finish courses quickly—it's to build genuine competence. Resist the temptation to use AI to generate solutions for challenges you haven't attempted yourself.

When used responsibly, AI tools can be valuable learning companions. The key is maintaining an active, critical, and curious mindset throughout your learning journey.
