## --quiz--

### --question--

#### --text--

What is a tilemap, in one sentence?

#### --distractors--

A texture atlas where each tile is one named frame.

---

A graphics object that draws coloured rectangles in a grid.

---

A spritesheet that loops a fixed animation across a 2D grid.

#### --answer--

A 2D array of indices into a tileset image, used to compose levels by stamping repeating tiles.

### --question--

#### --text--

Which two `this.load.*` calls do you need to load a Tiled map plus its tileset?

#### --distractors--

`this.load.image` for the map and `this.load.atlas` for the tileset.

---

`this.load.tilemap` for the map and `this.load.image` for the tileset.

---

`this.load.json` for the map and `this.load.spritesheet` for the tileset.

#### --answer--

`this.load.tilemapTiledJSON` for the map and `this.load.image` for the tileset image.

### --question--

#### --text--

Inside `create`, what does `map.addTilesetImage("dungeon", "tiles")` actually do?

#### --distractors--

Loads the dungeon tileset image at runtime.

---

Renders the tileset onto a tile layer at depth `0`.

---

Returns a Phaser sprite sheet you can pass to `this.anims.generateFrameNumbers`.

#### --answer--

Binds the tileset named `"dungeon"` (as written in the Tiled JSON) to the loaded image keyed `"tiles"` in Phaser, and returns a tileset object you pass into `createLayer`.

### --question--

#### --text--

You call `walls.setCollision([1, 4, 7])`. What is the trade-off?

#### --distractors--

It collides on every tile *except* indices 1, 4, and 7.

---

It silently does nothing — `setCollision` only accepts a single integer.

---

It is faster but uses more memory than `setCollisionByProperty`.

#### --answer--

It works, but it is fragile — re-arranging the tileset shifts the indices and silently breaks every existing map.

#### --answer--

### --question--

#### --text--

A pillar tile is visually 16×16 but should only block the lower 12×8. Where should the tighter hit-box live?

#### --distractors--

Hard-coded in `update` via per-frame `body.setSize` calls on every pillar.

---

In a separate JSON file the runtime overlays at load time.

---

It cannot be done in Phaser — you must split the pillar into two tiles.

#### --answer--

In Tiled's per-tile **Tile Collision Editor** — Phaser reads the collision rectangle from the JSON automatically.

### --question--

#### --text--

What is the difference between a **tile layer** and an **object layer** in a Tiled map?

#### --distractors--

Tile layers can be rendered; object layers can only carry data and never reach the screen.

---

Tile layers are static; object layers are always animated.

---

They are interchangeable — Phaser treats both as 2D arrays of indices.

#### --answer--

Tile layers describe terrain as a 2D grid of indices into a tileset; object layers describe entities and triggers as positioned objects with custom properties.

---

`map.objects.filter("torch")`

#### --answer--

`map.filterObjects("objects", o => o.type === "torch")`

### --question--

#### --text--

Why do you usually shift a Tiled tile-object's position by `+ 8` when rendering a Phaser sprite at it?

#### --distractors--

Tiled stores positions relative to the camera, not the world.

---

Phaser's tilemap layer adds an `8`-pixel padding around the map by default.

---

Phaser draws sprites at half-resolution, which doubles their effective offset.

#### --answer--

Tiled stores tile-object positions at the bottom-left corner; Phaser sprites are origin-centred by default, so adding half a tile (8 px on a 16-px tileset) re-centres them on the cell.

### --question--

#### --text--

A door object carries `properties: [{ name: "locked", value: true }]`. What's the safest way to read the boolean?

#### --distractors--

`d.locked === true` — Phaser always flattens custom properties.

---

`d.props.locked` — Phaser exposes them under a `props` field.

---

`map.getProperty(d, "locked")` — the engine's helper handles missing keys.

#### --answer--

`d.properties.find(p => p.name === "locked").value`, with a fallback to `d.locked` if the runtime has flattened them already.

### --question--

#### --text--

A polyline object's `polyline` field stores `[{x: 0, y: 0}, {x: 32, y: 0}, ...]`. Why is the first point `(0, 0)` even though the slime is in the middle of the room?

#### --distractors--

Tiled normalises every polyline to start at `(0, 0)` for compression.

---

Phaser strips the absolute origin during import.

---

The polyline tool stores world coordinates only when the layer has its origin at `(0, 0)`.

#### --answer--

Polyline points are stored relative to the object's origin (`obj.x`, `obj.y`); convert to world space with `obj.x + p.x`, `obj.y + p.y`.

### --question--

#### --text--

The slime patrol uses `Phaser.Math.Distance.Between(slime.x, slime.y, target.x, target.y) < 4`. What does the `< 4` accomplish?

#### --distractors--

It limits the slime to 4-tile patrol routes.

---

It prevents the slime from colliding with the player while patrolling.

---

It tells Phaser to ignore the slime for 4 frames after each waypoint.

#### --answer--

It treats "close enough" as arrival — without it the slime would jitter near each waypoint and never advance, because exactly hitting a floating-point coordinate is unrealistic.

### --question--

`walls.setCollisionAt(tx, ty, false)`

#### --answer--

### --question--

#### --text--

What does `this.cameras.main.setLerp(0.1)` do when following a player?

#### --distractors--

It clamps the camera to a 10-pixel margin around the player.

---

It tells the camera to update only every 10 frames.

It scales the camera viewport to 10% of its original size.

#### --answer--

### --question--

#### --text--

What is a minimap, in Phaser terms?

#### --distractors--

A built-in `MinimapPlugin` you enable in the game config.

---

A render-to-texture of the main camera, drawn at a smaller scale.

### --question--

#### --text--

Why launch the HUD as a *separate* scene (`UIScene`) instead of drawing it in the gameplay scene?

#### --distractors--

Phaser cannot render UI text in the same scene as a tilemap.

---

`localStorage` is per-scene, so saves only work when the HUD is isolated.

---

The arcade physics world conflicts with HUD rendering.

#### --answer--

It separates concerns — the HUD has its own depth space, can be paused/resumed independently, and can stay alive across gameplay scene restarts.

### --question--

#### --text--

What's the safest way to pass HP from gameplay to HUD?

#### --distractors--

A global `window.gameState` object, mutated from both scenes.

---

`this.scene.get("UIScene").drawHp(this.hp)` directly.

---

A custom DOM event dispatched on `document`.

#### --answer--

The gameplay scene emits `this.events.emit("ui:hp", this.hp)`, and the HUD listens with `main.events.on("ui:hp", (hp) => this.drawHp(hp))`.

### --question--

#### --text--

You restart the gameplay scene three times during testing and the dialogue typewriter starts firing two characters at a time. Why?

#### --distractors--

The typewriter timer is leaking memory and slowing down the page.

---

`this.time.delayedCall` resets the typewriter speed on each restart.

---

`localStorage` is caching a stale dialogue index.

#### --answer--

Each scene restart added a new `"ui:dialogue"` listener without removing the old ones — register a `this.events.on("shutdown", ...)` that calls `main.events.off(...)` for every event the HUD listens to.

### --question--

#### --text--

A `Set`-based inventory needs to survive a `localStorage` save. Which serialization round-trip works?

#### --distractors--

`JSON.stringify(set)` directly — `Set` has built-in JSON support.

---

Convert with `set.toJSON()` and restore with `Set.fromJSON(text)`.

---

Save the size and the items as separate keys, then re-create the set on load.

#### --answer--

`JSON.stringify([...set])` to save and `new Set(JSON.parse(text))` to load — `JSON` does not understand `Set`, but it understands arrays.
