# --description--

Mateo is mentioning how old he is in Spanish using the verb `tener` (to have) in the folloiwing structure:

The verb `tener` + number in words + `años`.

Remember, numbers in Spanish from 21 to 29 start with `veinti-`, followed by the unit number. Twenty is `veinte`, so twenty-five is `veinticinco` removing the `e` and adding `i` before the unit number `cinco`. Numbers from 1 - 9 in Spanish are:

- `uno` – one
- `dos` – two
- `tres` – three
- `cuatro` – four
- `cinco` – five
- `seis` – six
- `siete` – seven
- `ocho` – eight
- `nueve` – nine
