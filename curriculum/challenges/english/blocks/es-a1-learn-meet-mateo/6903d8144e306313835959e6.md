# --explanation--

In Spanish, the verb `tener` (to have) is used to express **age**. For example:

- `Tengo 25 años` - I am 25 years old.

- `Mi hermana tiene 22 años.` – My sister is 22 years old.  

To ask someone’s age in Spanish. For example:

`¿Cuántos años tienes?` – How old are you?

To answer, you can say:

- `Tengo 30 años.` – I am 30 years old.  

Breakdown of two elements in the sentence wrapping numeric age:

- `Tengo` - I have  

- `Años` - years

Remember the letter `ñ` in `años` has a tilde (`˜`), giving it a distinct sound.
