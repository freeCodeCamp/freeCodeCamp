# --interactive--

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the field of building computer systems that can perform tasks that normally require human intelligence. These tasks include understanding spoken or written language, recognising objects in photos, making decisions based on incomplete information, and translating between languages.

AI does not mean a robot or a sentient machine. It simply means software that can do something we would normally need a human brain to do.

### A Brief History

The idea of AI is older than computers themselves. As early as the 1950s, researchers were asking whether machines could think. In 1950, mathematician Alan Turing proposed a test — if a machine can hold a conversation indistinguishable from a human, it should be considered intelligent.

Early AI programs were built on hand-written rules. Developers would explicitly code every decision:

```
if the email contains "free money", mark it as spam
```

This approach works for simple problems, but breaks down quickly. You cannot write a rule for every possible photo, sentence, or situation in the world.

### Why AI Changed

The key shift happened when researchers stopped writing rules and started letting computers learn rules from data.

Instead of telling the machine "a cat has pointy ears and whiskers", you show it one million photos labelled "cat" or "not a cat" and let it figure out the patterns on its own. This approach — learning from examples — is called Machine Learning, and it is the foundation of all modern AI.

### AI in Everyday Life

AI is already embedded in tools you use every day. Your smartphone keyboard predicts your next word. Streaming services recommend what to watch. Email clients filter spam automatically. Maps estimate your arrival time. None of these systems were programmed with explicit rules for every situation — they all learned from data.

### Key Points

- AI is software that performs tasks requiring human-level intelligence
- Early AI relied on hand-coded rules, which did not scale
- Modern AI learns patterns from large amounts of data
- AI is already used in products you interact with every day
