# --interactive--

A 60×40 tilemap has 2,400 cells. A 200×200 has 40,000. The performance differences between *correct* and *naive* tilemap setup are large enough that you can feel them on a low-end laptop.

The single biggest win is **only enable collision on the layer that needs it**. A typical level has three layers: `floor`, `walls`, `decorations`. Only `walls` is solid. If you accidentally call `setCollisionByProperty` on `floor` (because every floor tile has a `kind: "floor"` property and your predicate is `(p) => p`), you have just made every floor cell a collidable body. The arcade physics broadphase now checks the player against every floor tile every frame.

```js
walls.setCollisionByProperty({ collides: true });
```

That's the line. `floor` and `decorations` get no collision call at all.

The second win is **culling**. Phaser's tilemap layers cull tiles outside the camera bounds for *rendering* automatically — that part is free. But if a layer is sparse (most cells empty), you can opt into not even iterating empty cells:

```js
walls.setCullPadding(2, 2);
walls.skipCull = false;
```

`setCullPadding` adds a margin around the camera view so tiles don't pop in at the edges. `skipCull = false` is the default; the only reason to set it explicitly is to remind future-you not to flip it to `true`.

:::interactive_editor

```js
const layer = {
  width: 200,
  height: 200,
  data: [],
  cameraBounds: { x: 100, y: 100, width: 30, height: 20 },
  padding: 2
};
const drawable =
  (layer.cameraBounds.width + 2 * layer.padding) *
  (layer.cameraBounds.height + 2 * layer.padding);
const total = layer.width * layer.height;
console.log("total tiles:", total);
console.log("drawable tiles after cull:", drawable);
console.log("cull ratio:", (drawable / total).toFixed(3));
```

:::

The third win is **don't recreate the map on level change**. If your game has multiple rooms in the same map, switching between them means switching cameras, fading, and updating physics bounds — *not* tearing down the tilemap and rebuilding it. Tilemap construction (especially the property scan) is cheap once but expensive every scene transition.

Anti-pattern to avoid: a per-frame `walls.setCollisionByProperty(...)` call inside `update`. The property scan is meant for `create`. Calling it every frame on a 60×40 map is ~2,400 property lookups × 60 fps = 144,000 wasted lookups per second. Phaser will let you do it; the framerate will not.

# --questions--

## --text--

Your top-down map has `floor`, `walls`, and `decorations` layers. The player is colliding with torches and floor tiles. What's the most likely cause?

## --answers--

`setCollisionByProperty` was called on the wrong layer (or with too loose a predicate that matches floor and decoration tiles too).

---

The tileset is corrupt.

### --feedback--

Corrupt tilesets fail to load entirely; selective collision is a logic bug.

---

The camera bounds are wrong.

### --feedback--

Camera bounds affect what's *drawn*, not what's solid.

---

The player has gravity enabled.

### --feedback--

Gravity moves the player; it doesn't decide which tiles are solid.

Where should `setCollisionByProperty` be called?

## --answers--

Once in `create`, on the layer that contains solid tiles.

---

Once per frame in `update`, to make sure the flags stay current.

### --feedback--

Per-frame calls re-walk the entire layer. The flags don't decay; one call in `create` is enough.

---

Once in `preload`.

### --feedback--

`preload` is for loading assets. The layer doesn't exist there yet.

### --feedback--

There's no such callback.

## --text--

What does `setCullPadding(2, 2)` do?

## --answers--

Extends the layer's culling rectangle 2 tiles past each side of the camera so tiles don't pop in as the camera scrolls.

---

Removes 2 tiles from each side of the layer to save memory.

### --feedback--

It changes culling, not the layer's tile data. Nothing is removed.

---

Adds a 2-pixel margin around the camera image.

### --feedback--

The argument is in *tiles*, not pixels.

---

Doubles the layer's tile size.

### --feedback--

Tile size is fixed by the tileset; cull padding doesn't touch it.
