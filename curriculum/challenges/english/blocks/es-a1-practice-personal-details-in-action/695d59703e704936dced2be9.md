# --description--

This task, is a basic question commonly used when meeting someone for the first time in Spanish.

In Spanish, many questions begin with words like `Cuál` (which), `Cómo` (how), `Quieres` (do you want), and `Puedes` (can you). 

These question words help form common and polite expressions such as `¿Cuál es tu nombre?` (What is your name?), `¿Cómo te llamas?` (What’s your name?), `¿Quieres ayuda?` (Do you want help?), and `¿Puedes repetirlo?` (Can you repeat it?). 

You might notice that Spanish often omits the subject pronoun `tú` (you). For example:

`¿Cómo te llamas?` instead of `¿Cómo tú te llamas?`. 

This is because Spanish verbs already include the subject in their conjugation, making the sentence clear and natural without needing to say `tú`.
