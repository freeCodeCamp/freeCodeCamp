# --interactive--

GANs have moved far beyond generating handwritten digits. They now power tools used in creative industries, medicine, gaming, and scientific research. This final lesson surveys the most important real-world applications and reflects on the broader implications of the technology you have built throughout this course.

### Image Synthesis and Creative Tools

**Face generation** — NVIDIA's StyleGAN series can generate photorealistic human faces that do not belong to any real person. The website thispersondoesnotexist.com demonstrates this: every face you see is entirely GAN-generated.

**Art and style transfer** — GANs like CycleGAN can translate images from one style to another without paired training data. A photograph can be rendered in the style of Van Gogh, or a horse can be transformed into a zebra, without any paired examples of horse-and-zebra images.

**Text-to-image generation** — early GAN-based text-to-image models (like StackGAN and AttnGAN) paved the way for modern diffusion-based systems. The text conditioning ideas developed in Conditional GANs directly influenced these systems.

### Data Augmentation and Synthetic Data

In many domains, labelled data is scarce and expensive to collect. GANs can generate synthetic training examples to supplement real data:

- **Medical imaging** — generating additional MRI scans or X-rays for rare conditions where only a handful of real examples exist
- **Autonomous vehicles** — generating rare driving scenarios (night, fog, unusual road conditions) that are difficult and dangerous to collect in real life
- **Manufacturing defect detection** — generating images of rare defects to train quality control systems when actual defective products are rare

### Super Resolution

Super-resolution GANs (SRGAN, ESRGAN) take a low-resolution image and generate a plausible high-resolution version. Unlike simple upsampling (which just blurs), a GAN-based approach generates fine detail:

```
Low resolution (64×64) → SRGAN → High resolution (256×256)
```

ESRGAN (Enhanced SRGAN) is used in commercial video upscaling software and gaming engines to enhance textures in real time.

### Video Generation and Deepfakes

GANs can be extended to video by treating temporal consistency as an additional constraint. Applications include:

- **Video prediction** — predicting future frames of a video
- **Video-to-video translation** — converting dashcam footage to a segmentation map in real time
- **Deepfakes** — synthesising a person's face onto another person's body in video

Deepfakes represent one of the most concerning applications of GANs. They are used maliciously for misinformation, non-consensual intimate imagery, and identity fraud. The same research community that builds GANs also builds GAN detectors — tools that can identify GAN-generated or manipulated media.

### Drug Discovery and Molecular Generation

Molecular structures can be represented as graphs, and GANs have been adapted to generate novel molecular graphs with desired chemical properties. Systems like MolGAN and REINVENT generate candidate drug molecules that are then evaluated for potential therapeutic value — dramatically accelerating the early stages of drug discovery.

### Ethical Considerations

GANs raise significant ethical concerns that every practitioner should understand:

- **Synthetic media and misinformation** — photorealistic fake images and videos can be used to spread false information
- **Privacy** — generating realistic images of real people without consent
- **Bias amplification** — if the training data contains biases (e.g., overrepresenting certain demographics), GANs will reproduce and potentially amplify those biases
- **Consent and attribution** — models trained on artists' work without permission raise questions about intellectual property

Responsible development means being aware of these concerns, advocating for transparency about synthetic media, and building detection tools alongside generative ones.

### Key Points

- GANs power photorealistic face generation, style transfer, super-resolution, and synthetic data generation
- Medical imaging and autonomous vehicle research use GAN-generated data to supplement scarce real-world datasets
- SRGAN and ESRGAN generate fine high-resolution detail from low-resolution inputs
- Deepfakes are a serious misuse of GAN technology with implications for misinformation and privacy
- Ethical GAN development requires awareness of bias, consent, and the potential for harm

How do GANs help address the problem of scarce labelled data in medical imaging?

## --answers--

By replacing the discriminator with a medical expert who labels generated images

### --feedback--

The discriminator is an automated neural network, not a human expert. GANs address data scarcity through a different mechanism that does not require additional human labelling.

---

By generating synthetic medical images to supplement the limited real examples available for rare conditions

---

By compressing existing medical images so more can be stored on the same hardware

### --feedback--

Image compression is a different technology. GANs address scarcity by creating new synthetic examples, not by making existing data smaller.

---

### --feedback--

GANs learn from the training data provided — they do not access the internet. The synthetic data they generate is created from learned patterns, not retrieved from external sources.

What makes SRGAN useful compared to simple image upsampling techniques?

## --answers--

SRGAN is faster than upsampling because it processes fewer pixels

### --feedback--

SRGAN is actually more computationally expensive than simple upsampling. Its value is in the quality of the output, not the speed.

---

SRGAN produces sharper images with realistic fine detail rather than blurry upsampled outputs

---

SRGAN can upscale images without requiring a GPU

### --feedback--

SRGAN, like most deep learning models, benefits significantly from GPU acceleration. The advantage over simple upsampling is about output quality.

---

SRGAN works exclusively on medical images and cannot process natural photographs

### --feedback--

SRGAN and ESRGAN are general-purpose and are widely used for natural photographs, gaming textures, and video. They are not restricted to medical imaging.

Which ethical concern is most directly associated with deepfake technology?

### --feedback--

While GPU energy consumption is a valid environmental concern for AI broadly, it is not the most direct ethical concern specifically associated with deepfakes.

Deepfakes can be used to spread misinformation and create non-consensual synthetic media of real people

---

Deepfakes make GAN training unstable and cause mode collapse

### --feedback--

Mode collapse is a technical training problem, not an ethical concern. The ethical issues with deepfakes relate to how the technology is used, not how it is trained.

---

### --feedback--

Accessibility of the technology is a separate concern from its ethical implications. The primary ethical concern is about the harm caused by synthetic media of real people.
