# --explanation--

The verb `tener` means **to have**, but it’s used in Spanish to express **age**. For example:

`Tengo treinta y seis años` - I am thirty-six years old.

To ask someone’s age in Spanish, you can say:

`¿Cuántos años tienes?` - How old are you?

How to use `tener` to talk about age. For example:

- `Tengo veinticinco años.` - I am twenty-five years old.

- `Mi hermano tiene treinta años.` - My brother is thirty years old.

The word `años` means **years**, and it has a tilde (~) over the “n”, changing the pronunciation from “n”.
