# --description--

There are many ways to represent data in a visual format. In this lesson, you will learn about a few commonly used types of data visualizations.

The first type you will learn about are charts that represent change over time, like stock price performance or health statistics.

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1759450056567/18f2b852-4b86-454f-b834-62eb85d43676.png align="center")

\[example area chart\]

Another common type of visual would be a category comparison chart. This type of visual shows comparisons between different categories like in ad performance across channels in marketing or sales by product category for online stores.

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1759450447086/fda4e7e3-5149-46ae-8589-3bfe5d50a8b7.png align="center")

\[example bubble chart for marketing channel performance leads\]

If you needed to represent an itemâ€™s position in an ordered list, you could use a ranking chart. Examples of this type of visual would include performance statistics or election results.

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1759450698635/9dffb64f-fea5-4c7d-8f66-6cdc14d1a0f9.png align="center")

\[example ranking chart for election results\]

Another commonly used type of data visualization would be a part of the whole visual. This would include pie charts or treemaps that could be used to represent budgets or product categories.

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1759451324835/13b82f62-8be5-43ce-a273-1d6d70123dd1.png align="center")

\[example pie chart for budget\]

If you are working with data the shows correlation between two or more variables, you can use a correlation chart. These types of charts would include heat maps or scatterplots that could represent data such as income vs. education level, advertising spend vs. sales, or temperature vs. energy consumption.

![](https://cdn.hashnode.com/res/hashnode/image/upload/v1759451646784/9b5f0743-ee49-4277-8594-88084e7caa1f.png align="center")

\[example scatterplot for income vs. education level\]

There are many more types of charts like flow charts, distribution charts, relationship charts and more. But this lesson was about providing you with a brief introduction into some of the types of charts you might consider when representing complex data.
