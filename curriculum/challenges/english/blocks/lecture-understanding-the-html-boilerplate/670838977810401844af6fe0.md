# --interactive--

Before writing a single line of code, it helps to see what deep learning can actually do. This lesson tours the major application areas — many of which you will build in this course.

### Computer Vision

Computer vision is the field of teaching machines to understand images and video. It was one of the first areas where deep learning dramatically outperformed all previous approaches.

Deep learning powers image classification ("Is this a cat or a dog?"), object detection ("Where are the people in this photo?"), medical imaging (detecting tumours in X-rays), autonomous vehicles (reading road signs and detecting pedestrians), and face recognition (unlocking your phone).

The breakthrough moment came in 2012 when a deep learning model called AlexNet won the ImageNet competition by a margin that shocked the research community. Computer vision has advanced rapidly ever since.

### Natural Language Processing

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is the field of teaching machines to understand, generate, and translate human language. Deep learning enables sentiment analysis, real-time machine translation, text summarisation, question answering, and the large language models behind tools like ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.

The transformer architecture — which you will study in Block 5 — is responsible for most of the recent leaps in NLP.

### Speech and Audio

Deep learning has transformed how computers process sound. Speech recognition converts spoken words to text (Siri, Google Assistant). Text-to-speech generates natural-sounding voice from text. Sound classification can identify a bird species from its song or detect machinery faults from audio.

### Reinforcement Learning

Reinforcement learning (RL) is a branch of deep learning where an agent learns by trial and error — receiving rewards for good actions and penalties for bad ones. In 2016, AlphaGo defeated the world champion at Go. AlphaZero later learned chess, shogi, and Go from scratch with no human game data in under 24 hours. RL is also used in robotics and optimising industrial processes.

### Healthcare and Science

AlphaFold (DeepMind, 2020) predicted the 3D structure of nearly every known protein, solving a 50-year-old biology problem. Deep learning is also used to screen drug candidates, match radiologist-level accuracy in detecting cancer from scans, and find patterns in DNA linked to genetic diseases.

### Generative AI

Generative models learn the patterns in a dataset and use them to create new content — photorealistic images from text prompts, working code from natural language descriptions, and short video clips from text. You will build your first generative model — a GAN — in Block 6.

### Key Points

- Computer vision was the first major win for deep learning, beginning with AlexNet in 2012
- NLP transformers power the large language models behind modern AI assistants
- AlphaFold used deep learning to solve a fundamental problem in biology
- Generative models can create new images, text, audio, and video from learned patterns
