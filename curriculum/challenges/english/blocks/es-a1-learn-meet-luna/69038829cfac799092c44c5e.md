# --description--

In this task, you’ll hear how Luna introduces herself in Spanish using the following structure:

`Me llamo` + name.

In Spanish, people don’t say **"I am called"** but rather **"I call myself"** when giving their name.

### --feedback--

# --explanation--

The phrase `me llamo Luna` it means **I call myself Luna**. For example:

- `me` - reflexive pronoun (myself)

- `llamo` - from the verb `llamar` (to call)

Together, `me llamo` is how you say your name in Spanish. For example:

`Me llamo Diego.` - My name is Diego.

Remember, the structure uses a **reflexive pronoun** (`me`) and the **verb** `llamar` in the first person form `llamo`.
