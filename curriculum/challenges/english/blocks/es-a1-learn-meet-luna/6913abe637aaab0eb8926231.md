### --feedback--

# --explanation--

The verb `tener` means **"to have."**. Its first-person singular form is `tengo`, meaning **"I have"** In Spanish, to express age, we say `tener` + number + `años` — literally **"to have X years"**. For example:

- `Tengo` 20 `años` – I am 20 years old.

- `Tengo` 35 `años` – I am 35 years old.  

So, `Tengo` 28 `años` translates to "I am 28 years old," even though it literally means "I have 28 years."
