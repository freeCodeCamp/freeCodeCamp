# --description--

In Spanish, there are two common ways to talk about origin. 

The first one is using **`Es de` + country**. For example:

`Es de Venezuela` - He is from Venezuela.

The second one is using the nationality adjective. For example:

`Es venezolano` - He is venezuelan.

Nationalities are **not** capitalized.

# --explanation--

Esteban says `Sí, es brasileño`, which means "Yes, he is Brazilian" in male form.

It's very important to include the accent mark in the letter `i` of the word `Sí`. With the accent mark, this word means "yes". Without the accent mark, based on the context, this word can mean "if", as a conditional.

Remember that nationalities are not capitalized in Spanish.
