# --interactive--

Every Arcade body exposes two parallel sets of edge flags: `body.touching` and `body.blocked`. They look identical at a glance — both have `up`, `down`, `left`, and `right` booleans — but they answer different questions.

- `body.touching.down` — "did this body collide with **another body** on its lower edge during the most recent physics step?"
- `body.blocked.down` — "did this body get pushed against the **world bounds** on its lower edge during the most recent physics step?"

That distinction matters for one specific question every platformer asks: *can the player jump?* If you check only `touching.down`, the player on the floor of the world (with no static tile under them) cannot jump. If you check only `blocked.down`, the player on a static platform cannot jump. The robust check uses both:

```js
function update() {
  const onGround = this.player.body.touching.down || this.player.body.blocked.down;
  if (Phaser.Input.Keyboard.JustDown(this.keys.up) && onGround) {
    this.player.setVelocityY(-360);
  }
}
```

:::interactive_editor

```js
function update() {
  const body = this.player.body;
  const onGround = body.touching.down || body.blocked.down;
  if (Phaser.Input.Keyboard.JustDown(this.keys.up) && onGround) {
    this.player.setVelocityY(-360);
  }
}
```

:::

Both flag sets are reset to all-`false` before each `update`. They reflect *only* the most recent step. So you cannot read `body.touching.down` in a callback fired three ticks later and get a meaningful answer — capture state at the moment you care about it.

The other thing to know: `touching` is the result of the collision, not its trigger. The flags get set during collision resolution, after `physics.world.step` runs but before your scene's `update`. By the time your `update` reads them, they are accurate for "this frame, what just happened." That is the basis for coyote time (lecture coming up) — you sample `touching.down` every frame and remember the timestamp of the last `true`.

# --questions--

## --text--

What is the difference between `body.touching.down` and `body.blocked.down`?

## --answers--

`touching.down` fires when this body collided with another *body* below it; `blocked.down` fires when it was pushed against the world's lower *bound*.

---

`touching` is for static bodies, `blocked` is for dynamic.

### --feedback--

Static-vs-dynamic does not split these flags. `blocked` is specifically about world bounds.

---

`touching` is checked once per frame, `blocked` is checked continuously.

### --feedback--

Both are reset at the start of each step and computed inside the same physics tick.

---

There is no difference; they are aliases.

### --feedback--

They are not aliases — they answer different questions.

## --text--

A player should be able to jump from either a platform tile *or* the world floor. Which check is correct?

## --answers--

`if (body.touching.down || body.blocked.down) jump();`

---

`if (body.touching.down) jump();`

### --feedback--

Misses the case where the player is on the world floor with no tile underneath.

---

`if (body.blocked.down) jump();`

### --feedback--

Misses the case where the player is standing on a platform tile (no world bounds involved).

---

`if (body.touching.down && body.blocked.down) jump();`

### --feedback--

Both being true at once is the rare case — the player is on a tile that sits on the world floor.
