### --question--

Which property on the Phaser game `config` sets the world gravity that every dynamic body inherits?

#### --distractors--

`config.physics.world.gravity`

`config.gravity.y`

---

`config.physics.gravity.y`

#### --answer--

`config.physics.arcade.gravity.y`

### --question--

#### --text--

You set world gravity to `y: 800` and want one specific body to ignore it. What is the cleanest way?

#### --distractors--

Set the world gravity to `0` and use `body.gravity.y = 800` on every other body.

---

Add the body to a static group.

---

Call `physics.world.disable(body)` and re-enable it later.

#### --answer--

Set `body.allowGravity = false` on that single body.

### --question--

#### --text--

What does a `body.bounce` of `1` mean in arcade physics?

#### --distractors--

The body cannot move.

---

The body bounces off the screen edges only.

---

The body's velocity is doubled on every contact.

#### --answer--

The collision is perfectly elastic — outgoing speed equals incoming speed.

### --question--

#### --text--

You give a body `setDrag(40, 0)`. What does it do?

#### --distractors--

It scales the body's gravity by `40`.

---

It sets the body's mass to `40`.

---

It pulls the body toward the point `(40, 0)`.

#### --answer--

It scrubs `40` units per second from the body's horizontal velocity each frame, while leaving vertical velocity untouched.

### --question--

#### --text--

When should you reach for `physics.add.overlap` instead of `physics.add.collider`?

#### --distractors--

When the bodies are both static and never move.

---

When you want the bodies to slide past each other only on the X axis.

---

When you want Phaser to call `setVelocity(0, 0)` on contact.

#### --answer--

When you want the callback to fire while the bodies intersect, but you do not want Phaser to separate them — pickups, damage zones, level-complete triggers.

### --question--

#### --text--

What does the **process callback** (the 4th argument to `physics.add.collider`) actually do?

#### --distractors--

Schedules the collision to fire on the next frame instead of this one.

---

Replaces the default arcade resolver with a custom one.

---

Stops Phaser from emitting a `worldbounds` event for the collision.

#### --answer--

Acts as a yes/no filter — return `true` to let the contact happen, `false` to skip it without resolving.

### --question--

#### --text--

What's the canonical way to make a one-way platform using a process callback?

#### --distractors--

Return `true` only when the player is moving leftward.

---

Return `false` whenever the player's body is touching another platform.

---

Return `true` only when the platform is moving.

#### --answer--

Return `true` only when the player's body bottom is above the platform's top (e.g. `player.body.bottom <= platform.body.top + 2`).

### --question--

#### --text--

Why are bodies in a `physics.add.staticGroup()` essentially free, performance-wise?

#### --distractors--

They share a single physics body across all members.

---

They are batched into one draw call by Phaser.

---

They never collide with anything.

#### --answer--

The physics engine never integrates velocity, applies gravity, or resolves penetration on them — collisions are resolved on whatever dynamic body hit them.

### --question--

#### --text--

You scale a static sprite *after* adding it to a static group, but the player phases through where the new edge should be. What's wrong?

#### --distractors--

Static groups do not support sprites that are not 1×1 scaled.

---

You must call `staticGroup.refreshBody(sprite)` instead — `body.refresh()` is for dynamic bodies.

---

Phaser silently ignores `setScale` on static sprites; you must rebuild the group.

#### --answer--

The collision rectangle was captured at the original size — call `body.refresh()` after the scale change so the body matches the new visual.

### --question--

#### --text--

You want a "moving platform" that rides a tween path and supports the player on it. Should it go in a static group?

#### --distractors--

Yes — static groups can be tweened and the body will follow.

---

Yes — moving platforms are always static; only obstacles are dynamic.

---

No — make it a dynamic body without `immovable` so the player pushes it sideways.

#### --answer--

No — make it a dynamic body with `body.allowGravity = false` and `body.immovable = true` (or use a `physics.add.group({ allowGravity: false, immovable: true })`).

### --question--

#### --text--

What does `body.setSize(10, 14)` do, on a sprite that was loaded from a 16×16 spritesheet frame?

### --question--

#### --text--

What is the difference between `body.touching.down` and `body.blocked.down`?

#### --distractors--

`touching` is for sprites; `blocked` is for rectangles.

---

`touching` is set in `update`; `blocked` is set in `preupdate`.

---

They are aliases — Phaser keeps both for backward compatibility.

#### --answer--

`touching.down` is true when the body is in contact with another body on its bottom side; `blocked.down` is true when the body is in contact with the world bounds on its bottom side.

### --question--

#### --text--

What's the **point** of coyote time in a platformer?

#### --distractors--

It speeds the player up briefly after leaving a moving platform.

---

It slows the camera by a few frames so jumps feel smoother.

---

It is a leftover term from Mario games and has no functional effect today.

#### --answer--

### --question--

#### --text--

How do you implement coyote time in `update(time)`?

#### --distractors--

Tween `body.velocity.y` toward `0` for the first `120` ms after a jump.

---

Schedule a `setTimeout` whenever the player touches ground that re-enables jumping for `120` ms.

---

Use `body.onFloor()` and ignore `update`.

#### --answer--

### --question--

#### --text--

What problem does **jump buffering** solve?

#### --distractors--

It batches multiple `update` calls into one to save CPU.

---

It debounces accidental double-jumps caused by sticky keyboards.

---

It clamps the jump button so it cannot fire more than once per second.

#### --answer--

A jump pressed *just before* landing should still fire the moment the player touches ground, instead of being dropped because the body was airborne at the press frame.

### --question--

#### --text--

You implement coyote time and jump buffering, then notice a single press fires *two* jumps when the player is mid-air at the press and lands inside both windows. What fixes it?

#### --distractors--

Increase the coyote window so the second window cannot fit.

Switch from `JustDown` to `isDown` for the jump key.

---

Disable jump buffering whenever coyote time is active.

#### --answer--

Reset `lastGroundedAt` and `lastJumpPressedAt` to `-Infinity` immediately after firing the jump, so neither window can fire it again.

### --question--

#### --text--

Which is the right way to implement **variable jump height** (tap = short hop, hold = full jump)?

#### --distractors--

Multiply `body.velocity.y` by `0.5` every frame the player is rising.

---

Set `body.gravity.y` to `2000` while the player holds jump.

---

Stamp the press time and apply a stronger jump velocity for longer-held presses.

#### --answer--

When `Phaser.Input.Keyboard.JustUp(cursors.up)` fires while the player is still rising, clamp `body.velocity.y` to a smaller-magnitude negative number (e.g. `-120`).
