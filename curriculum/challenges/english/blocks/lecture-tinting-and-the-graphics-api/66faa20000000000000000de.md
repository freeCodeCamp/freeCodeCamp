# --interactive--

You don't need to repaint your art every time the player gets hit. Phaser lets you re-color a sprite at runtime by passing a tint to its texture sampler. The cost is essentially free ŌĆö one shader uniform per object ŌĆö and the visual punch is the difference between a flat hit and a satisfying one.

The simplest call is `setTint(color)`:

```js
this.player.setTint(0xff3366);  // pink-ish red
```

The number is a hex color in `0xRRGGBB` form: `0xff0000` for pure red, `0x00ff00` for pure green, `0xffffff` for white (no visible change against the original art). The shader multiplies each pixel by that color, so dark areas stay dark, light areas pick up the tint.

A few useful variants:

- `setTint(color)` ŌĆö uniform tint across the whole sprite.
- `setTint(topLeft, topRight, bottomLeft, bottomRight)` ŌĆö gradient tint, one color per corner. Useful for shading characters as they walk into shadows.
- `setTintFill(color)` ŌĆö replaces every pixel with the tint color, ignoring the original art. The sprite becomes a flat silhouette in that color. Use for hit-flash or "frozen" effects.
- `clearTint()` ŌĆö restore the original colors.

The classic "got hit" effect:

```js
enemy.setTintFill(0xffffff);
this.time.delayedCall(80, () => enemy.clearTint());
```

For 80 ms the enemy is a white silhouette, then snaps back. Combine that with a tween, a screen shake, and a sound, and you've taught the player that the hit registered.

A few constraints worth knowing:

- Tints work on **textured** game objects: `Sprite`, `Image`, `TileSprite`. They do not apply to `Rectangle`, `Text`, or `Graphics` (those have their own color APIs).
- Tints multiply, so tinting an already-dark sprite barely changes anything. If you need bright recolor, your source art needs lightness in it.
- Tinting requires WebGL on hot paths. Canvas mode supports tints but pays a per-frame copy cost, so don't tint hundreds of objects per frame in Canvas.

Tints are also one of the cheapest ways to differentiate variants of the same sprite. A 4-color enemy team ŌĆö red, green, blue, yellow zombies ŌĆö can share one sprite sheet plus four `setTint` calls, no extra art.

# --questions--

## --text--

Which Phaser API should you use to flash an enemy white for 80 ms when it's hit?

## --answers--

`setTintFill(0xffffff)` followed by `clearTint()` after a delay.

---

`setTint(0xffffff)` followed by `clearTint()` after a delay.

### --feedback--

`setTint(0xffffff)` multiplies by white ŌĆö i.e. no visible change. `setTintFill` is what replaces every pixel.

---

`setVisible(false)` followed by `setVisible(true)` after a delay.

### --feedback--

That hides the sprite, which reads as "broken" rather than "hit."

---

`destroy()` followed by re-spawning the sprite.

### --feedback--

Destroying and respawning loses physics state and is far more expensive.
