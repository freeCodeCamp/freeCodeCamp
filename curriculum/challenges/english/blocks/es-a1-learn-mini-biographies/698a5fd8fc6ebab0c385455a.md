### --feedback--

This is the verb `tener` (to have) conjugated for the third person singular ("he"/"she"). Remember that in Spanish, you "have" years.

# --explanation--

Spanish uses the verb `tener` ("to have") to express age. For example: 

`Él tiene veinte años` - He is twenty years old.

When describing someone in a professional context, it's common to combine the verb `ser` ("to be") for the profession, with the verb `tener` ("to have") for the age. For example:

`Es diseñador gráfico` (profession) + `y tiene veintiséis años` (age).
