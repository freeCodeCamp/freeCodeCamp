# --interactive--

A `tileSprite` is a `Sprite` that **tiles a single texture across a fixed area**. You give Phaser one image and a width/height, and Phaser repeats the image as many times as needed to fill the rectangle. As you scroll the texture, the image *appears* to move forever without ever leaving the rectangle.

```js
this.background = this.add.tileSprite(240, 160, 480, 320, "sky");
```

`add.tileSprite(x, y, width, height, key)` is the standard signature. `(x, y)` is the **center** of the tile sprite (just like `add.image`). `(width, height)` is the visible rectangle. `"sky"` is a texture loaded in `preload`. If the texture is smaller than the rectangle, Phaser tiles it; if larger, only the centered slice is shown (you usually don't want this â€” match texture size to your tiling needs).

The magic is `tilePosition`:

```js
this.background.tilePositionX += 0.4;
```

This shifts the tiled texture inside the rectangle. The rectangle stays fixed at `(240, 160)`. The *contents* of the rectangle scroll. From the player's perspective, the sky appears to drift even though the game object hasn't moved. That's the entire trick of cheap, infinite scrolling backgrounds.

You can also set `tileScale`:

```js
this.background.tileScaleX = 2;
```

Doubles each tile's width inside the rectangle. Useful when your texture is too small to look right at 1x but you don't want to re-export it.

:::interactive_editor

```js
const rect = { width: 480, tileTextureWidth: 256 };
const tilePosX = 0;
let scrollX = tilePosX;
const speed = 0.4;

const frames = [];
for (let i = 0; i < 8; i += 1) {
  scrollX += speed * 60;
  frames.push({
    frame: i,
    tilePositionX: scrollX,
    visibleWrap: scrollX % rect.tileTextureWidth
  });
}

console.log("scrolled tile positions over 8 frames:");
for (const f of frames) console.log(f);
```

:::

When **does** `tileSprite` give you trouble?

1. **Non-tileable images.** If the texture has hard edges that don't repeat seamlessly, you'll see a visible seam every `texture.width` pixels of scroll. Either author the texture as tileable (left edge meets right edge) or place a continuous gradient over the seam.
2. **Pixel snapping.** At fractional `tilePositionX`, the texture interpolates and you may get a blurry seam on pixel-art games. Use integer increments (or `Phaser.Math.Snap.To(tilePositionX, 1)`) for crisp pixels.
3. **`setScrollFactor` interaction.** A `tileSprite` *is* a game object; it scrolls with the camera by default. To get parallax, you set its scroll factor and animate its `tilePositionX` together â€” covered in the next lecture.

Mental model: a `tileSprite` is a window onto an infinitely-wide repeated texture. The window stays put on the canvas; you scroll the texture inside the window. That's all.

## --text--

`this.add.tileSprite(240, 160, 480, 320, "sky")` â€” what do `(240, 160)` represent?

## --answers--

The center position of the tile sprite on the canvas (its anchor for `(0.5, 0.5)`).

---

The top-left corner.

### --feedback--

`tileSprite` uses the same origin as `Sprite` and `Image` â€” center by default.

---

The texture offset within the tile sprite.

### --feedback--

That's `tilePositionX/Y`. The first two arguments place the rectangle on the canvas.

---

The size of one tile.

### --feedback--

Tile size comes from the texture; `(width, height)` is the rectangle, `(x, y)` is its center.

## --text--

What does increasing `tilePositionX` do?

## --answers--

Shifts the tiled texture leftward inside the visible rectangle, which looks like the texture scrolling left.

---

Moves the tile sprite right on the canvas.

### --feedback--

The tile sprite stays where you placed it. Only the texture *inside* it moves.

---

Stretches the texture wider.

### --feedback--

That's `tileScaleX`, not `tilePositionX`.

---

Adds a new tile to the right.

### --feedback--

Tiles aren't added; the texture is shifted and wraps automatically.

## --text--

You see a hard vertical seam every few seconds in your scrolling sky. What's the most likely cause?

## --answers--

The sky texture isn't horizontally tileable â€” its left edge doesn't match its right edge.

---

You forgot to set `setScrollFactor(0)`.

### --feedback--

That would cause the sky to scroll with the camera, not produce a seam.

---

`tilePositionX` is incrementing too fast.

### --feedback--

Speed produces faster scrolling, not seams. Seams come from the texture's edges.

---

Pixel-art textures can't be used in tile sprites.

### --feedback--

They can be, and often are. The seam is about edge continuity, not pixel art specifically.
