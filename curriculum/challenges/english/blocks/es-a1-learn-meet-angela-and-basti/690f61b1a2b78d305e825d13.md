# --description--

Now you'll notice something new at the end of this sentence.

`¿Y vos?` is an alternative, informal way of returning a question to the original speaker. It's equivalent to `¿Y tú?` ("And you?").

This is commonly used in Central America, in countries like El Salvador and Guatemala, and in countries from the Southern Cone, like Argentina and Uruguay.

# --explanation--

`Soy de El Salvador` means "I am from El Salvador". Ángela uses the common structure `Soy` ("I am") + `de` ("from") to say where she is from.

`Soy salvadoreña` means "I am Salvadoran". Ángela uses the feminine form (`salvadoreña`) because she is a female.

`¿Y vos?` is a regional way to return the question. It's equivalent to "And you?".
