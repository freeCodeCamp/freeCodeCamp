## --quiz--

### --question--

#### --text--

Why does loading a single sprite sheet outperform loading every animation frame as a separate file?

#### --distractors--

Sprite sheets compress more aggressively than individual PNGs.

---

Sprite sheets render at a higher resolution than individual files.

---

Sprite sheets bypass the browser's HTTP cache.

#### --answer--

The browser fetches one file and the GPU uploads one texture, instead of one of each per frame.

### --question--

#### --text--

What's the main difference between a sprite sheet and a texture atlas?

#### --distractors--

Atlases ship as JPEG; sprite sheets ship as PNG.

---

Atlases are exclusive to 3D engines; sprite sheets are 2D-only.

---

Atlases support animation; sprite sheets do not.

#### --answer--

Sprite sheets use a fixed grid of equally-sized frames; atlases let frames be any size at any position, indexed by name in a JSON manifest.

### --question--

#### --text--

In `this.load.spritesheet("hero", url, { frameWidth: 16, frameHeight: 16 })`, what does `frameWidth: 16` mean?

#### --distractors--

---

---

#### --answer--

Each cell on the sheet is 16 pixels wide; Phaser slices the sheet into rectangles of that size.

### --question--

#### --text--

A red `ADD`-blended sprite is laid over a white background. The result reads as white, not red. Why?

#### --distractors--

The sprite's depth is too low; it's hiding under the background.

---

`ADD` only works on pure black backgrounds.

---

Phaser disables `ADD` over light backgrounds for accessibility.

#### --answer--

`ADD` clips at 1.0 per channel; white already saturates every channel, so the result reads as white.

### --question--

#### --text--

You want a glowing ring to follow the player without redrawing every frame. What's the most efficient pattern?
