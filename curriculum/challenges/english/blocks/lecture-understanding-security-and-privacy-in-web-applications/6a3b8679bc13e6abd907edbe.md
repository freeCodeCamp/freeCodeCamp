# --description--

**Security protects data from unauthorized access, theft, and malicious damage.** It builds a strong digital wall around your information online.

Think of data security as a heavy lock on your front door. The lock keeps intruders out of your house. In the digital world, security focuses on safeguarding data confidentiality, data integrity, and system availability.

Cybersecurity tools include network firewalls, strong passwords, data encryption, and antivirus software. If an outside hacker tries to steal your files, security systems block the attempt.

Security does not analyze what the data means. It only focuses on keeping the data safe from bad actors. Without security, any data you store online remains vulnerable to immediate theft.

**Privacy controls how organizations collect, store, use, and share your personal data.** It gives you the power to dictate who sees your private information.

Look at data privacy as a window curtain. Even if your window has a strong lock, people can see inside if you leave the curtain wide open.

Privacy ensures that companies respect your personal rights. It dictates whether a company can sell your browsing history, track your GPS location, or share your email address with third-party advertisers.

Global laws like the GDPR from the European Union (EU) strictly regulate data privacy. You will learn about those laws in a coming lecture.

Now let's look at the relationship between security and privacy. Though they are distinct concepts, they closely depend on each other.

You can easily have security without having privacy. For instance, a smartphone manufacturer might store your daily location data in a highly secure cloud database. Advanced hackers cannot breach this server, so the data remains entirely safe from bad actors online.

However, if the manufacturer sells that data to advertising agencies without your explicit permission, then your privacy is compromised. The security works perfectly, but your data privacy is zero.

On the other hand, you can never achieve true privacy without robust security. If a medical clinic promises never to share your health records, they are promising privacy. But if the clinic fails to secure their local servers, a rogue hacker can easily steal those medical records.

This complete lack of security instantly destroys your data privacy. Therefore, robust security acts as the essential foundation for effective privacy.

Security provides the technical defense mechanism, while privacy governs the legal rights and policies concerning data usage. Understanding this difference helps you navigate the digital space safely.

Security teams build technical defenses against active cyberattacks, while privacy teams ensure compliance with privacy laws and respect user choices.

Both elements remain essential to protect individuals online today. Security locks the front door, and privacy draws the window blinds. Together, they create a safe, trustworthy environment for your personal information.
