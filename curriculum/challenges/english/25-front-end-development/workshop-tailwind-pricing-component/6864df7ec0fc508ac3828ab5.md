# --description--

From regular CSS, you learned that assigning a display of `grid` to an element does not do anything yet until you specify the grid rows and columns. THat's how it is in Tailwind too.

In Tailwind, to assign a grid row and grid column, you'd use their respective utility classes this way:

```html
<div class="grid grid-cols-[num] grid-rows-[num]">
  <div>Grid Item</div>
  <div>Grid Item</div>
  <div>Grid Item</div>
</div>
```

Assign the wrapping `div` the utility classes `grid-cols-1` and `md:grid-cols-3`.

`grid-cols-1` will give the three items in the `div` a single column, so all of them go top down, while `md:grid-cols-3` will assign them three columns with each item aligned horizontally on medium and larger devices.
